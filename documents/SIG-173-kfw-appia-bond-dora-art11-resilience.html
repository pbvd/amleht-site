03 · DORA Article 11 · What Was Proven
DORA Article 11 governs ICT-related incident management and the resilience of business continuity arrangements. Article 17 covers the supervisory notification assessment. Both apply when an issuer mid-lifecycle moves between ICT third parties at the registrar, chain, and payment-rail layers. Appia attests both in a single live event.
DORA Art.11(1)
ICT business continuity policy must include arrangements for the protection and recovery of ICT systems. Appia demonstrates the recovery posture against simultaneous failure of three independent ICT third parties.
DORA Art.11(2)
Tests of business continuity plans must include scenarios for cyberattack, switch-over from primary to backup, and recovery from severe disruption. The three-break configuration tests precisely the third category.
DORA Art.11(6)
Financial entities shall draw lessons learned from ICT-related incidents. Appia produces an immutable, chain-attested lesson set per break — readable by both internal risk teams and the supervisor without dependency on the migrated infrastructure.
DORA Art.17 (Threshold)
Incident classification — does this event require supervisory notification? The chain produces the dual-stream evidence package needed for the threshold assessment without ad-hoc reconstruction from logs.
